Numerical Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 3x3
Player 1 uses odd numbers (1, 3, 5, 7, 9) and Player 2 uses even numbers (2, 4, 6, 8) and each number can only be used once. A player wins by placing 3 numbers in a row, column, or diagonal that add up to exactly 15.

5x5 Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 5x5
Players take turns placing their mark in an empty square until only one square remains empty. This results in a total of 24 moves (12 for each player).The Player with more aligning 3 of his mark wins.

Word Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 3x3
The first player to form a valid three-letter word horizontally, vertically, or diagonally wins. The game is a draw if the board is filled without a valid word being formed.

SUS Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 3x3
Player 1 uses 'S' and player 2 uses 'U'. A player scores a point for each "S-U-S" sequence they create. The game ends when the board is full. The player who has created the most "S-U-S" sequences wins.

Connect 4 Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 6x7
Each Player place his mark in the bottom-most square in this column.The first player to get four of their marks in a row horizontally, vertically, or diagonally wins.

Obstacles Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 6x6
After every round (one turn for each player), two new obstacle cells are randomly added to the board. These cells cannot be used by either player. The first player to align 4 of their marks in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) wins. The game is a draw if the board fills without a winner.

Memory Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 3x3
Whenaplayerplaces a mark, it is immediately hidden from view. Players must remember the positions of all marks to plan their strategy.The first player to align three of their hidden marks in a row wins. The game is a draw if the board fills without a winner.

Infinity Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 3x3
After every three moves, the oldest mark on the board disappears. The first player to align three marks in a row before any of those marks vanish wins the game.

Diamond Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 5x5
A player wins by simultaneously completing a line of 3 marks and a line of 4 marks. The two lines must be in different directions (e.g., one horizontal, one diagonal) but can share one common mark.

Pyramid Tic-Tac-Toe

Chess Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 4x4
Rules:Players take turns moving one of their tokens to an adjacent (horizontal or vertical) empty square. Tokens cannot move diagonally or jump over other tokens. The first player to align three of their tokens in a row, column, or diagonal wins.

Easy Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe

Board => 9x9
Players take turns playing in any cell. The winner of a smaller board claims that corresponding cell on the main board with their symbol. The first player to win three smaller boards in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) on the main 3x3 grid wins the game.
